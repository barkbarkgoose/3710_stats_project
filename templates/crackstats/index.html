Hardware and Password Cracking

  • On a modern computer (8 core, 2.8 GHz) using the SHA512 hashing algorithm, it takes about 0.0017 milliseconds to compute a hash. This translates to about 1.7 * 10 ^ -6 seconds per password.
  • A GPU or 3D graphics card can usually performs operations at a speed of 50-100 times relative to the speed of a CPU.
  • We'll say that a supercomputer or botnet is able to perform calculations and hashes at a speed of 100000 times a regular computer. (you have 100000 computers working at the same time)
    • 100k computers on a botnet is very doable, the largest one to-date is estimated to have 12 million

  • ** on average the password will be cracked when half of the possible permutations are checked. (divide calculated number of possible permutations by two)
